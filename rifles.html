Assault Rifles Collection
AK-47 (Avtomat Kalashnikova 1947)

The AK-47, officially known as the Avtomat Kalashnikova, is an assault rifle that is chambered for the 7.62×39mm cartridge. Developed in the Soviet Union by Russian small-arms designer Mikhail Kalashnikov, it is the originating firearm of the Kalashnikov family of rifles.

Action: Gas-operated, long-stroke piston, closed rotating bolt

Caliber: 7.62×39mm

Muzzle velocity: 715 m/s (2,350 ft/s)

Feed system: 20-round, 30-round, 50-round detachable box magazine, 40-round, 75-round drum magazines also available

Type: Assault rifle

Country of Origin: Soviet Union (now Russia)

Year Introduced: 1947 (Soviet Union)

M16

The M16 rifle is a family of assault rifles adapted from the ArmaLite AR-15 rifle for the United States military. The original M16 rifle was a 5.56×45mm automatic rifle with a 20-round magazine.

Action:air-cooled, gas-operated, magazine-fed assault rifle, with a rotating bolt.

Caliber: 5.56×45mm NATO

Muzzle velocity: 3,150 ft/s (960 m/s) (M855A1 round)

Magazine: 20/30-round detachable magazine

Type: Assault rifle

Country of Origin: United States

Year Introduced: 1964 (United States)

FN SCAR

The FN SCAR is a family of gas-operated short-stroke gas piston automatic rifles developed by Belgian manufacturer FN Herstal in 2004. It is constructed with modularity for the United States Special Operations Command to satisfy the requirements of the SCAR competition.

Action: Gas-operated short-stroke piston, rotating bolt

caliber: Available in 5.56×45mm NATO (SCAR-L) and 7.62×51mm NATO (SCAR-H)

Magazine: 20/30-round detachable magazine

Muzzle velocity: SCAR-H: 870 m/s (2,900 ft/s); SCAR-L: 710 m/s (2,300 ft/s)

Type: Assault rifle / Battle rifle

Country of Origin: Belgium (FN Herstal)

Year Introduced: 2004 (Belgium)

Heckler & Koch G36

The Heckler & Koch G36 is an assault rifle designed in the early 1990s by German weapons manufacturer Heckler & Koch. It is chambered in 5.56×45mm NATO, and replaced the heavier G3 battle rifle chambered in 7.62×51mm. The G36 was accepted into service with the Bundeswehr in 1997.

Action: Short-stroke piston, closed rotating bolt

Caliber: 5.56×45mm NATO

Magazine: 30-round detachable magazine

Type: Assault rifle

Country of Origin: Germany

Year Introduced: 1997 (Germany)

AR-15

An AR-15–style rifle is a lightweight semi-automatic rifle based on or similar to the Colt AR-15 design. The Colt model removed the selective fire feature of its predecessor, the original ArmaLite AR-15, which is a scaled-down derivative of the AR-10 design. It is closely related to the military M16 rifle.

Action: Gas-operated, rotating bolt

Caliber: 5.56×45mm NATO or .223 Remington

Magazine: Detachable box magazine, 20 or 30 rounds

Type: Semi-automatic rifle (civilian version of the M16)

Country of Origin: United States

Year Introduced: 1959 (United States)

AR-15

The AR-15 is a lightweight, air-cooled, gas-operated, selective fire rifle chambered for the 5.56×45mm NATO round. It was originally designed in the 1950s and is widely used in both civilian and military applications, with the M16 being its military version.
