Thompson M1921 (Tommy Gun)

The Thompson submachine gun (also known as the "Tommy gun", "Chicago typewriter", or "trench broom") is a blowback-operated, selective-fire submachine gun, invented and developed by Brigadier General John T. Thompson, a United States Army officer, in 1918.

Action: Blowback, selective fire

Caliber:.45 ACP

Type: Classic Submachine Gun (SMG)

Magazine Capacity: 20, 30, or 50 rounds (stick or drum magazine)

Country of Origin: United States

Year Introduced:1921

MP 40

The MP 40 (Maschinenpistole 40) is a submachine gun chambered for the 9×19mm Parabellum cartridge. It was developed in Nazi Germany and used extensively by the Axis powers during World War II.

Caliber: 9×19mm Parabellum

Type: Classic Submachine Gun (SMG)

Magazine Capacity: 32 rounds

Country of Origin: Germany

Year Introduced:1940

Beretta M12

The Beretta M12 (Model 12) is a 9×19mm Parabellum caliber submachine gun designed by Beretta. Production started in 1959, the first users were the Italian Carabinieri, Italian State Police and the Guardia di Finanza, though in limited numbers; it was only widely issued beginning in 1978, replacing the old Beretta MAB.

Action: Blowback, selective fire

Country of Origin: Italy

Year Introduced: 1960
