Pistol Collection
Glock 17

The Glock 17 is a short recoil–operated, locked-breech semi-automatic pistol that uses a modified Browning cam-lock system adapted from the Hi-Power pistol.

Action: Short recoil, locked breech

Caliber: 9mm Parabellum

Type: Semi-automatic, striker-fired

Magazine Capacity: 17 rounds

Country of Origin: Austria

Year Introduced: 1982

Beretta 92

The Beretta 92 (also Beretta 96 and Beretta 98) is a series of semi-automatic pistols designed and manufactured by Beretta of Italy. The Beretta 92 was designed in 1975, and production began in 1976. Many variants in several calibers continue to be used to the present.

Action: Short recoil, locked breech

Caliber: 9mm Parabellum

Type: Semi-automatic, double/single action

Magazine Capacity: 15 rounds

Country of Origin: Italy

Year Introduced: 1976

Colt 1911

The Colt 1911 is a semi-automatic pistol that fires the . 45 ACP (Automatic Colt Pistol) cartridge. It uses a recoil-operated action to fire rounds. The pistol has a single-action trigger, meaning the hammer must be manually cocked before firing each shot.

Action: Short recoil, locked breech

Caliber: .45 ACP

Type: Semi-automatic, single action

Magazine Capacity: 7-8 rounds

Country of Origin: United States

Year Introduced: 1911

Sig Sauer P226

The SIG Sauer P226 is a full-sized service pistol made by SIG Sauer. This model is available in four chamberings: the 9×19mm Parabellum, .40 S&W, .357 SIG, or .22 Long Rifle.

Action: Short recoil, locked breech

Caliber: 9mm Parabellum (.40 S&W and .357 SIG also available)

Type: Semi-automatic, double/single action

Magazine Capacity: 15-20 rounds (varies by caliber and magazine)

Country of Origin: Germany/USA (produced by Sig Sauer)

Year Introduced: 1984

Heckler & Koch USP

The USP (Universelle Selbstladepistole or "universal self-loading pistol") is a semi-automatic pistol developed in Germany by Heckler & Koch GmbH (H&K) as a replacement for the P7 series of handguns. Detachable box magazine; capacities: 15 rounds (9x19mm Parabellum)

Action: Short recoil, locked breech

Caliber: .40 S&W, 9mm Parabellum, .45 ACP

Type: Semi-automatic, double/single action

Magazine Capacity: 10-15 rounds

Country of Origin: Germany

Year Introduced: 1993

Smith & Wesson M&P

The Smith & Wesson M&P is a polymer-framed, short recoil operated, locked breech semi-automatic pistol introduced in the summer of 2005 by the American company Smith & Wesson. It uses a Browning-type locking system. While targeted at law enforcement agencies, the M&P is also widely available on the commercial market.

Action: Short recoil, locked breech

Type: : Semi-automatic, striker-fired

Country of Origin: United States

Year Introduced: 2005

CZ 75

The CZ 75 is a semi-automatic pistol made by Czech firearm manufacturer ČZUB. First introduced in 1975, it is one of the original "wonder nines" and features a staggered-column magazine, all-steel construction, and a hammer forged barrel.

Action: Short recoil, locked breech.

Caliber: 9mm Parabellum

Type: Semi-automatic, double/single action

Magazine Capacity: 16-19 rounds

Country of Origin: Czech Republic

Year Introduced: 1975

FN Herstal FNX-45

Steyr M

The Steyr M is a series of semi-automatic pistols developed by Steyr Mannlicher GmbH & Co KG of Austria for police services and the civilian shooting market. Design work on the new pistol began in the early 1990s and the final product known as the M9 was officially unveiled in the spring of 1999.

Action: Short recoil, locked breech

Caliber: 9mm Parabellum

Type: Semi-automatic, striker-fired

Magazine Capacity: 17 rounds

Country of Origin: Austria

Year Introduced: 1999

Browning Hi-Power

The Browning Hi-Power is a single-action, semi-automatic pistol available in the 9×19mm Parabellum and .40 S&W calibers. It was based on a design by American firearms inventor John Browning, and completed by Dieudonné Saive at FN Herstal. Browning died in 1926, several years before the design was finalized.

Action:Short recoil, locked breech

Caliber: 9mm Parabellum, .40 S&W (some models)

Type: Semi-automatic, single action

Magazine Capacity: 13-15 rounds

Country of Origin: Belgium

Year Introduced: 1935

SIG Sauer P320

The SIG Sauer P320 is a modular semi-automatic pistol made by SIG Sauer, Inc., SIG Sauer's American branch. It is a further development of the SIG Sauer P250, utilizing a striker-fired mechanism in lieu of a double action only hammer system.

Action: Short recoil, locked breech

Caliber: 9mm Parabellum, .40 S&W, .45 ACP

Type: Semi-automatic, striker-fired

Magazine Capacity: 17-21 rounds

Country of Origin: United States (assembled in Germany)

Year Introduced: 2014

Makarov PM

The Makarov pistol or PM is a Soviet semi-automatic pistol. Under the project leadership of Nikolay Fyodorovich Makarov, it became the Soviet Union's standard military and Militsiya side arm in 1951.

Action: Blowback

Caliber: 9mm Makarov (9x18mm)

Type: Semi-automatic, double/single action

Magazine Capacity: 8 rounds

Country of Origin: Soviet Union (now Russia)

Year Introduced: 1951
